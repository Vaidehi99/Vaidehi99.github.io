About Me

I am Vaidehi Patil, a final year student pursuing an Interdisciplinary Dual Degree: B. Tech. in Electrical Engineering and M. Tech. in AI and Data Science and a Minor Degree in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT Bombay. My research interests include Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Sunita Sarawagi from IIT Bombay and Dr. Partha Talukdar from Google Research India. I have interned at Adobe Research India in Summer 2020 and Summer 2021. I’ll be joining the Computer Science Department at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill as a PhD student in Fall 2022. I’ll be advised by Prof. Mohit Bansal and will be a part of the MURGe-Lab and the broader UNC-NLP group.

Publications

Vaidehi Patil, Partha Talukdar, Sunita Sarawagi
May, 2022 In ACL 2022
Overlap-based Vocabulary Generation Improves Cross-lingual Transfer Among Related Languages

Propose Overlap BPE (OBPE), an effective modification to the BPE vocabulary generation algorithm which enhances overlap across related languages. Unlike previous studies that dismissed the importance of token-overlap, we show that in the low-resource related language setting, token overlap matters.

Detecting Document Versions and Their Ordering In a Collection

Propose a three-stage approach for the task of identifying versions and ordering them correctly in this paper and show that it significantly outperforms state-of-the-art approach adapted for this task, which justifies defining this problem as a novel challenge.

August, 2021 In ACL 2021
Exploiting Language Relatedness for Low Web-Resource Language Model Adaptation: An Indic Languages Study

Experiments on multiple real-world benchmark datasets provide validation to our hypothesis that using a related language as pivot, along with transliteration and pseudo translation based data augmentation, can be an effective way to adapt LMs for LRLs, rather than direct training or pivoting through English.
